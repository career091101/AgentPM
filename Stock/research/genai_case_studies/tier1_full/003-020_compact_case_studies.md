- **Finance/Banking**: 7 (Deutsche Bank, Société Générale, BNP Paribas, ING, Santander, Lloyds, Swiss Re)
- **Insurance**: 3 (Aviva, Munich Re, Swiss Re)
- **Tech/Software**: 2 (SAP, Nokia, Ericsson)
- **Consumer**: 3 (Adidas, Zalando, Spotify)

### LLM Platform Breakdown
- **ChatGPT/OpenAI**: 12 cases (Bosch, Philips, Ericsson, Deutsche Bank, BNP Paribas, Adidas, Santander, Lloyds, Swiss Re, Aviva-partial)
- **Claude**: 5 cases (Siemens, ASML-implied, Philips-testing, Zalando, Munich Re-indirect)
- **In-House/Partnership**: 3 cases (Société Générale, ING, Nokia-NVIDIA)

### Common Success Patterns
1. **Domain-Specific Fine-Tuning**: Each company customized LLM for industry (finance data, manufacturing parameters, medical records)
2. **Human-in-the-Loop**: Critical decisions retain human oversight (reducing hallucination risk)
3. **Governance First**: Companies established AI ethics boards before scaling
4. **Speed as Competitive Advantage**: AI shortened cycles 3-10x (SAP invoices, Zalando imagery, ING chatbot)
5. **Cost Reduction Focus**: Average 30-75% labor cost savings across all cases

### Common Challenges
1. **Regulatory Uncertainty**: EU AI Act implications still evolving
2. **Talent Shortage**: Lack of AI-skilled workforce in Europe
3. **Legacy System Integration**: Old infrastructure incompatible with AI pipelines
4. **Data Privacy**: Tension between AI model training and GDPR compliance
5. **Vendor Lock-In**: Dependency on US LLM providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, NVIDIA)
