# Instacart - On-Demand Grocery Delivery Scorecard (Apoorva Mehta)

**Tier 2 Case Study for `/startup-scorecard` Skill**

## Summary
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Founder | Apoorva Mehta |
| Company | Instacart |
| Founded | 2012 |
| Valuation | $39B (2021) |
| **Scorecard** | **38/50** ⭐⭐⭐ |

## Key Scores
- Market (8/8): Grocery delivery $400B+ TAM
- CPF (8/10): 78% - Grocery shopping is time-consuming
- PSF (8/10): 10x time savings (1 hour → 5 minutes ordering)
- Business Model (7/10): LTV/CAC 3x (high shopper costs)
- Execution (7/10): Operations-heavy, requires local scaling
- Uniqueness (8/10): Retailer partnerships (vs. inventory-based competitors)

## 10x Advantage
- **Time Savings**: 10x faster (order in 5 min vs. 1 hour shopping)
- **Selection**: Access to multiple stores (vs. single store visit)
- **Convenience**: 1-hour delivery (vs. next-day competitors)

## Rapid PMF Achievement
- Launched: 2012
- 1000 orders/week: 6 months
- 10K orders/week: 18 months
- Fastest grocery delivery to PMF (vs. competitors: 3-5 years)

## Business Model Challenge
- Revenue: 5-10% service fee + markup
- CAC: $50 (ads, promotions)
- LTV: $150-200 (avg 10 orders × $15-20 profit)
- LTV/CAC: 3x (below VC strict standard 5x)

## Lesson: Unit Economics Trade-off
- Grocery delivery: Low margins (10-15%)
- Result: LTV/CAC 3x (vs. SaaS 5-8x)
- **However**: Massive TAM ($400B) compensates for lower margins
- Scorecard adjustment: Market size bonus (+1 point)
