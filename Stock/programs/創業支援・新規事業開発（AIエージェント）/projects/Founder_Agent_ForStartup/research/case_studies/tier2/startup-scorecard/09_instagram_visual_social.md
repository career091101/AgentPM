# Instagram - Visual Social Network Scorecard (Kevin Systrom)

**Tier 2 Case Study for `/startup-scorecard` Skill**

## Summary
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Founder | Kevin Systrom, Mike Krieger |
| Company | Instagram |
| Founded | 2010 |
| Exit | $1B (Facebook, 2012) |
| **Scorecard** | **40/50** ⭐⭐⭐ |

## Key Scores
- Market (8/8): Social media $500B+ TAM
- CPF (8/10): 80% - Mobile photo sharing pain point
- PSF (9/10): **Filters = 10x better photos instantly**
- Business Model (7/10): No revenue (pre-acquisition), but exceptional growth
- Execution (8/10): Pivot from Burbn (check-in app) to Instagram
- Uniqueness (9/10): Network effects, simplicity (vs. feature-heavy competitors)

## 10x Advantage
- **Photo Quality**: Filters make amateur photos look professional (10x)
- **Speed**: Upload in seconds (vs. Flickr's minutes)
- **Simplicity**: Photo-only (vs. Facebook's text/links/events)

## Growth Metrics
- 1M users in 2 months (fastest to 1M at the time)
- 30M users in 18 months (acquired by Facebook)
- DAU/MAU: 70%+ (high engagement)

## Pivot Success
- **Original**: Burbn (location check-in + photos + gamification)
- **Pivot**: Instagram (photos only, removed everything else)
- **Trigger**: Users only used photo feature
- **Scorecard**: Burbn 20/50 → Instagram 40/50 (+20 points)

## Lesson: Simplicity Wins
- Removed features (check-in, gamification)
- Focused on one thing (photo sharing + filters)
- Result: 10x faster growth
