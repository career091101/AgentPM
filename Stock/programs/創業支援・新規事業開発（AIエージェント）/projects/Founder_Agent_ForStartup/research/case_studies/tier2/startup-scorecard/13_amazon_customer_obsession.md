# Amazon - Customer Obsession Scorecard (Jeff Bezos)

**Tier 2 Case Study for `/startup-scorecard` Skill**

## Summary
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Founder | Jeff Bezos |
| Company | Amazon |
| Founded | 1994 |
| Market Cap | $1.7T (2024) |
| **Scorecard** | **46/50** ⭐⭐⭐ (Legendary tier) |

## Key Scores
- Market (8/8): E-commerce $5T+ TAM
- CPF (9/10): 90% - Book buying inconvenience (pre-internet)
- PSF (10/10): ⭐⭐⭐ **Unlimited selection, 1-click ordering**
- Business Model (9/10): Flywheel model, exceptional LTV/CAC
- Execution (10/10): ⭐⭐⭐ Operational excellence, Day 1 culture
- Uniqueness (10/10): ⭐⭐⭐ Network effects, AWS platform moat

## 10x Advantage (Multiple Axes)
- **Selection**: 10x more books than physical stores
- **Convenience**: 1-click vs. driving to store
- **Price**: 10-30% cheaper (no retail overhead)
- **Delivery**: 2-day (Prime) vs. immediate (store) - initially worse, later 10x better with same-day

## Amazon Flywheel
```
Lower Prices → More Customers
     ↓              ↑
More Sellers  →  Selection
     ↓              ↑
Better Experience ← Traffic
```

## Business Model Evolution
**Phase 1 (1994-2000)**: Books only
- LTV/CAC: 2x (low, but building infrastructure)

**Phase 2 (2000-2006)**: Marketplace + AWS
- LTV/CAC: 5x (marketplace = low CAC)

**Phase 3 (2006+)**: Prime + Ecosystem
- LTV/CAC: 10x+ (Prime members: $1400 LTV vs. $600 non-Prime)

## Scorecard Evolution
- **1994** (Seed): 25/50 (unproven concept)
- **2000** (IPO): 35/50 (profitable growth)
- **2010** (AWS launch): 42/50 (platform expansion)
- **2020** (Current): 46/50 (ecosystem dominance)

**Impact on Scorecard**:
- Execution feasibility: 10/10 (culture = execution)
- Long-term thinking: Multi-decade planning
- Day 1 mentality: Always act like a startup

## Quantitative Benchmark: Prime Membership
- Prime members: 200M+ worldwide
- Spend: $1400/year (vs. non-Prime: $600/year)
- Retention: 93% annual renewal
- **Result**: LTV $7000+ (5-year horizon)

## Lesson for `/startup-scorecard`
**Perfect Score (46/50) Requires**:
1. Massive TAM ($1T+)
2. Multiple 10x advantages (selection, price, convenience)
3. Flywheel/network effects
4. Operational excellence (Bezos's leadership)
5. Long-term vision (20+ year planning)

**Very few companies achieve 45+ score** - Amazon, Airbnb, Google, Facebook
